# Programmatic: 4-Stage Operating System Build

Purpose: Defines implementation lifecycle, ownership levels, and delivery timeline.


## 1. Fulfillment Framework
- **DIY (Do It Yourself)**: Client provides data/access; we provide the mapping frameworks.
- **DWY (Done With You)**: Collaborative feedback loops, Slack-integrated design, and stakeholder training.
- **DFY (Done For You)**: Technical implementation, automation builds, and asset creation.

---

## 2. The Build Lifecycle

### Stage 1: Document & Discover (2–4 Weeks)
- **Ownership**: **DIY** (Client-led data extraction)
- **Actions**: Audit existing SOPs, process maps, and tool stacks (NMS/CRM/Billing). Identify "Stuck Ticket" patterns and regional inconsistencies.
- **Outputs**: 
  - Red/Yellow/Green Infrastructure Report.
  - Systems map of current workflows.
  - Initial Tool & Asset inventory.

### Stage 2: Research & Design (2–3 Weeks)
- **Ownership**: **DWY** (Collaborative Design)
- **Actions**: Architect the "Operating Backbone" around existing tools. Use local LLM workflows to analyze edge cases and bottlenecks.
- **Outputs**: 
  - Proposed systems architecture.
  - Prioritized workflow backlog.
  - Implementation roadmap for automations and portals.

### Stage 3: Build & Document (≈4 Weeks)
- **Ownership**: **DFY** (Programmatic Implementation)
- **Actions**: Deploy automation layers (Make.com/n8n) across intake, onboarding, and support. Build the "Loom Library" for internal training.
- **Outputs**: 
  - Live automation ecosystem.
  - Video walkthrough library (Loom).
  - Internal Playbooks and system change-logs.

### Stage 4: Testing, Training & Handoff (2–3 Weeks)
- **Ownership**: **DWY / DIY** (Stakeholder Testing)
- **Actions**: Live stress testing with actual ISP operators. Refine escalation paths. Assign RACI-style ownership for the new system.
- **Outputs**: 
  - Fully tested system (First 10 customers move through error-free).
  - Complete offboarding packet.
  - Optional: Transition to Fractional Ops Retainer.

---

## 3. The Operational Guarantee
We consider a project "Live" only when your team is autonomous. If your team cannot run the workflow by the end of Stage 4, we remain in your Slack until they can—guaranteeing that your first 10 customers move through the new system without manual errors.
